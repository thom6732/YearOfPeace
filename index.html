The Practices

Year of Peace is a collection of simple grounding practices. They are free. They are practical. Use them as needed.

Practice 01 • Reset

The 60-Second Reset

  1. Stand up. If you cannot stand, sit on the edge of your chair.
  2. Plant both feet flat on the floor. Push your heels down until you feel your leg muscles engage.
  3. Drop your shoulders. Physically pull them down away from your ears.
  4. Unclench your jaw. Leave a gap between your upper and lower teeth.
  5. Take three breaths. Focus only on the sensation of the air hitting the back of your throat.
  6. Resume work.
Practice 02 • Sensation

The Grip & Release

  1. Hold a grounding object (stone or keychain) in your dominant hand.
  2. Squeeze it as hard as you safely can for a count of five. Feel the edges press into your palm.
  3. Release the grip instantly on the count of six.
  4. Notice the difference in sensation between the "Grip" state and the "Release" state.
  5. Repeat three times.
  6. This signals your nervous system that the threat has passed.
Practice 03 • Movement

Walking Pace

  1. As you walk, synchronize your breathing with your steps.
  2. Inhale for 3 steps.
  3. Exhale for 3 steps.
  4. Once that rhythm is steady, extend the exhale to 4 steps.
  5. Do not stop walking. If your mind wanders, return your attention to the count: In, 2, 3. Out, 2, 3, 4.
Practice 04 • Shock

Temperature Grounding

  1. Find a source of cold. Cold water, a cold metal surface, or the Metal Mantra Card.
  2. Place the cold source against the pulse point on your wrist or the side of your neck.
  3. Hold it there for 30 seconds.
  4. Describe the sensation to yourself using neutral words (e.g., "It is sharp," "It is numbing," not "It is freezing/bad").
  5. This forces the brain to prioritize physical input over mental noise.
Practice 05 • Boundary

The Threshold (End of Day)

  1. Choose a physical threshold (a car door, a front door, a hallway arch).
  2. Before crossing it, stop.
  3. Place your hand on the frame of the door.
  4. Visualize leaving the urgency of the day on the outside of that line.
  5. Take one deep breath.
  6. Cross the threshold. You are now in a different place.

Use what's useful. Ignore the rest.
